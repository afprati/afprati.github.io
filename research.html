Research

annamariaprati afprati







My work broadly uses computational social sciences and mixed methods to study the intersection of peace, security, and political economy. My dissertation project examines how the international community supports local institutions in fragile states.

You can find my published paper "A Multi-Task Gaussian Process Model for Inferring Time-Varying Treatment Effects in Panel Data" from AIStats (with Yehu Chen, Jacob Montgomery, and Roman Garnett). We have a working paper version for the social sciences audience.

Here is a selection of my other working papers:

  • Building State Capacity Locally: International Interventions and Local Governance in Fragile States (Job Market paper)
  • Beyond Peacekeepers: The United Nations Development Programme and Non-Militarized Violence Mitigation (Under review; awarded best poster from APSA 2024)
  • Using Large Language Models for Custom Event Datasets (with Jacob Montgomery)
  • A Gaussian Process Framework for Structured, Flexible, and Interpretable Machine-Learning Models (with Yehu Chen, Jacob Montgomery, and Roman Garnett)
  • Border Fortifications and Trust in Government (with David Carter, Beth Simmons, and Michael Kenwick)
  • Modelling Cycles of Violence in Israel and Palestine (with Carly Wayne)
