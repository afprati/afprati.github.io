Hello! I am a doctoral candidate in Political Science at Washington University in St. Louis and on the academic job market. My work uses computational social sciences and mixed methods to study the intersection of peace, security, and political economy.

My dissertation and book project uses mixed methods to study the effect of peacebuilding projects led by the United Nations Development Programme on local governance in fragile states around the world. My job market paper examines how UNDP partnered with village residents to prevent rent-seeking in a local governance project during the Nepali civil war. The project includes semi-structured interviews I conducted during field work in Nepal, regression analyses using a novel dataset I compiled on the projects, and a Bayesian machine learning model to assess the dynamic impacts of the intervention. My work has been supported by Washington University's Center for Race, Ethnicity, and Equity, the Weidenbaum Center, and the Institute for Humane Studies.

Prior to attending WashU, I graduated Phi Beta Kappa with interdisciplinary honors from Stanford University, earning a bachelor's degree in international realtions. I also have master's degrees from the University of Chicago and Columbia University, as well as experience working in the data science industry. You can find my CV here.
