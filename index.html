I am a doctoral candidate in Political Science at Washington University in St. Louis, studying International Relations and Methods.

My dissertation and book project uses mixed methods to study how international actors support fragile states, with a particular focus on the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP). As part of this project, I collected two novel datasets on UNDP's work and did field work in Nepal, conducting semi-structured interviews in the Kathmandu Valley and in rural Lumbini Province.

Methodologically, I specialize in developing new tools that support the study of International Relations, including a Gaussian Process model for difference-in-differences and a project to dynamically and generically create event datasets from any text corpus.
